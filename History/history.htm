From where Bulgaria started.


Pliska and Khan Asparuh

Centuries ago, the land that would come to be known as Bulgaria was a crossroads of civilizations.
Its history began with the arrival of the Bulgar tribes in the 7th century.

Led by Khan Asparuh, they crossed the Danube River and established the First Bulgarian Empire in 681 AD.
The city of Pliska became its capital, marking the birth of Bulgarian statehood.

Under the Bulgar rulers, the empire flourished and expanded its territory.
The Bulgarians assimilated with the local Slavic and Thracian populations, creating a unique fusion of cultures.
